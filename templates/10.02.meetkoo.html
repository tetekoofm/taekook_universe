Who is Jeon Jungkook?

⬇️

🐻 Meet Jeon Jungkook 🐯

Jeon Jungkook, (전정국), better known as Jungkook, is a South Korean singer, songwriter and record producer. He is the youngest member of the K-Pop band BTS. He is the lead vocalist, center and maknae of the group. He is called The Golden Maknae of BTS, because of his powerhouse vocals, passionate performance and creative spirit. His journey from a shy Busan boy to a global icon is filled with growth, resilience, and golden charm.

“I would rather die than live without passion” — JK
⬇️

🌱 From Busan to BigHit

Born on September 1, 1997, in Busan, South Korea, Jeon Jungkook was a quiet and curious child. He graduated from Seoul’s School of Performing Arts in 2017. He received his college degree in Broadcasting and Entertainment in 2022. He grew up with a deep love for drawing, gaming, and sports — never quite imagining he’d one day stand on the world’s biggest stages.

“Respect, Understanding, Consideration. A person should always have these three.” — JK

In 2011, he auditioned for Superstar K at just 13 years old. Though he didn’t make it to the finals, his talent caught the eye of multiple entertainment companies but, he chose Big Hit because of RM. Jungkook trained hard — undergoing dance training in LA, mastering vocals, and growing through every stage of practice. When BTS debuted in 2013, he was just 15 but, his energy, voice, and sharp gaze made him unforgettable. From a shy boy who barely spoke to the group’s “Golden Maknae,” Jungkook’s transformation was as inspiring as it was beautiful. Every stage, every note, every step — he gave his all.

🎤 The BTS Years

V made his debut with BTS on June 13, 2013. He was credited for music composition on many BTS songs, first being ‘Hold Me Tight’ from the album ‘The Most Beautiful Moment in Life, Part 1.” The first solo single he composed for BTS was ‘Stigma’ from the album “Wings”. His second solo song with BTS, ‘Singularity,’ was released in May 2018, on their third LP, “Love Yourself: Tear.” ‘Singularity earned critical acclaim, including being ranked #20 on the “65 Best Songs of 2018’ by the New York Times.

As a solo artist, he released his first self-composed song, ‘Scenery’ on January 30, 2019 via SoundCloud. It broke the daily streaming record nine times during its first two weeks of release. Seven months later, he released ‘Winter Bear’ on August 9, 2019, along with a self-directed music video published on BTS’s YouTube page.

He followed those up with ‘Sweet Night’ in March 2020, a song he wrote and performed for the soundtrack of the JTBC drama ‘Itaewon Class.’ ‘Sweet Night’ debuted at number two on Billboard’s U.S. Digital Song sales chart, the highest debut for a K-soloist in the chart’s history. Then on December 25, 2020, he released another solo track, ‘Snow Flower’ featuring Peakboy. He released his third OST on December 21, 2021 with ‘Christmas Tree.’ It was written for the South Korean television series ‘Our Beloved Summer.’

“Sometimes, it is okay to take a break, take a step back and reflect on yourself.” — V

👑 The Golden Maknae

Beyond the stage, Jungkook’s love for sports shines just as brightly. Whether it’s boxing, running, playing sports, or dominating at the Idol Star Athletics Championships, his athleticism has always been natural and effortless. His powerful, focused energy in training, from gym workouts to boxing sessions, shows a side of him that's disciplined, fierce, and unstoppable.

As BTS rose to global stardom, Jungkook grew with them, not just as the youngest member, but as a powerhouse performer who earned the nickname “Golden Maknae.” Why? Because he could do it all. Dance. Sing. Rap. Perform. Direct. Produce. Even box and paint, if you gave him a chance. He is also a black belt in Taekwondo, a testament to his strength, discipline, and endless drive to master everything he touches.

“Effort makes you. You will regret someday if you don't do your best now. Don't think it's too late to keep working on it.” — JK

🐶 Meet: Bam

Jungkook’s loyal Doberman, Jeon Bam, made his first public appearance in 2021 during BTS’s reality show In the SOOP. Since then, Bam has become an icon in his own right.

Jungkook's bond with Bam is evident through his posts and live videos where he often talks and shares moments with Bam. He even created a dedicated Instagram account for Bam, sharing videos of Bam's life. Jungkook has also mentioned that Bam is primarily scared of unfamiliar men.

As Jungkook said, “Bam is not just my dog. He’s family.” That bond has captured fans’ hearts, showing us the kind of warmth that lives behind the stage lights.

“He listens to me so well. He is a BIG baby boi and he stole the show for me. I miss him” — JK

🎨 The Artist Within

Behind the spotlight, he’s a quiet creator who sees beauty in small moments and turns them into art. His music videos reflect his vision, with visuals that deepen the stories he tells. Through his role in BTS’s creative work, he’s become an artist who not only performs but also builds the narratives behind each piece. His Golden Closet Films, also known as GCF, are not simple travel videos. They are memories, carefully shaped with emotion and attention. Every frame, from the angle of the shot to the choice of color, carries meaning and intention.

He has a deep love for painting, using it as another way to express his feelings. He finds comfort in cooking for the people he cares about. In one of his many live streams, he cooked perilla oil makguksu and shared his recipe, which went viral! His passion for cooking even led him to choose the role of a chef during his military service. He doesn’t just express; he leaves pieces of his heart in everything he does.

Jungkook is constantly exploring new forms of artistic expression. His solo releases like “Euphoria” and “My Time” showcase his growth as an artist. His directorial touch came through in BTS’s “Life Goes On” music video, where he captured quiet moments of healing and togetherness during uncertain times.

“Effort makes you. You’ll regret someday if you don’t try your best now. Don’t think it’s too late but keep working on it. It may take time but there’s nothing that gets worse when you practice. You may get sad, but it’s evidence that you are doing good.” — JK

✨ The Voice of JK: Solo Era

Jungkook released his first solo album, “GOLDEN.” on November 3, 2023.

The album includes 11 tracks that showcase his range — from emotional ballads to energetic pop hits. Inspired by the golden moments of love, Jungkook poured his heart into an album that reflects both his growth and his dreams.

Tracklist:

  • 3D (feat. Jack Harlow)
  • Closer to You (ft. Major Lazer)
  • Seven (ft. Latto)
  • Standing Next to You
  • Yes or No
  • Please Don’t Change (ft. DJ Snake)
  • Hate You
  • Somebody
  • Too Sad to Dance
  • Shot Glass of Tears
  • Seven (Clean Ver.)

‘Seven’ was the lead single, released in July 2023, followed by ‘3D’ in September 2023. ‘Standing Next to You’ is the title track.

Each song painted a new facet of Jungkook. Through stages on global platforms like the Global Citizen Festival and his “Golden Live On Stage” in Seoul, he proved his global star power. From stripped-down acoustic moments to high-energy performances, he embodied every note. GOLDEN wasn’t just an album — it was a declaration: Jungkook, the artist, had arrived.

Some of the achievements the album received are:

  • The first K-pop soloist to chart 3 songs simultaneously (from the same album) in the Billboard Hot 100 Top 50.
  • First South Korean Soloist To Earn A BRIT Silver Certification For An Album.
  • First album by a K-pop Soloist to surpass 3 million sales in Hanteo history.
“I want to put the things that best represent me now on this album.” — JK

🎧 Golden Hours with JK

Jungkook connects with fans in the most genuine, unscripted ways. Whether it’s 3AM karaoke sessions, gym vlogs, or casual Weverse Lives, he shares slices of his real life with comforting honesty. His lives often stretch for hours — singing, chatting, laughing, or just vibing in silence with ARMY. He even made a Weverse Live record with over 20M views & over 290M likes!

From reading fan comments to performing song requests live, Jungkook treats each moment like a personal hangout. He reassures fans with his warmth and sincerity — often telling them to eat well, sleep more, and love themselves. Whether he’s playful or quietly reflective, every live feels like a golden hour shared together.

“ARMY, you give me strength. Let’s be together for a long, long time.” — Jungkook
